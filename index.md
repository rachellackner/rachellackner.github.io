I am a PhD candidate in Chemistry in the lab of [Professor David M. Chenoweth](http://chenowethgroup.chem.upenn.edu/index.html){:target="\_blank"} at the University of Pennsylvania. My research focuses on the synthesis and optimization of small molecule probes and fluorescent dyes for studying biological systems with spatiotemporal control.

Before coming to Penn, I earned my undergraduate degree from Yale University, where I worked in the lab of [Professor Scott J. Miller](https://millerlab.yale.edu/){:target="\_blank"} on the synthesis of phosphothreonine-based peptide catalysts. Outside of the lab, I was a writer and editor for *The Yale Record.*

My work with organic dyes has led me to an interest in the intersection of chemistry and art conservation. You can find me at the microscope or contact me at **rlackner@sas.upenn.edu.**
